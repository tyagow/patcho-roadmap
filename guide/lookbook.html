01

The Name Is The Secret Weapon

"Patcho" looks simple. It isn't. It sits one phonetic step from two different words — and they both point straight at this brand. That triangulation is something almost no name gives you.

PATCH
English

The iron-on / sew-on patch — a physical badge you collect and stitch onto everything. Also a patch of dirt (the yard, the trail), and to patch = repair the lip after rain. The merch object and the dig-ethic, in one word.

PARCHE
Latin-Am. Spanish

El parche = your crew and the spot where the crew gathers. Parchar = to hang with your people. A dirt jump literally is a patch of packed dirt a crew gathers around. The name means "the crew."

-CHO
PT-BR / familiar

Warm, nickname-shaped, in the friendly family of muchacho, gaúcho, poncho — earthy, Latin, rugged-but-kind. No negative meaning in Portuguese, English, or major Spanish. Clean runway.

The unlock

The logo can literally be a patch. That makes your product, your identity, and your loyalty program the same object. Sell a patch → you sold membership. Every owner of a Patcho patch is part of el parche. Few brands on earth get to collapse those three things into one cheap, high-margin, infinitely-collectible token.

Keep "Born in the yard. Built on dirt." as the hero line. Add "Find your patch" and "Somos Patcho" as the crew companions.

Handle note: bare @patcho is taken. Claim one variant identically everywhere — @ridepatcho (front-runner), @somospatcho, or @wearepatcho + matching .com. (Formal trademark clearance still needed before filing.)

Four moods, all set in real fonts that are free for commercial use. One note from the research: Thrasher's font (Banco) is trademark-adjacent — so for grunge, distress Anton rather than copy Banco.

06

Voice & Taglines

Sounds like a friend handing you a shovel, not a brand handing you a catalog. Short, dirt-under-the-nails, warm where the scene is usually cold — because this came from a home and a couple. Bilingual and unbothered about it.

The line system

Born in the yard. Built on dirt. Find your patch. If you ride, you dig. Dirt is home. Dirt don't lie. Pack it. Send it. Patch it. Shovels up. One crew. One patch. Earn your patch. Somos Patcho. Nascido no quintal. Feito na terra. Quem anda, cava. Terra é casa. Bora pro parche.

✓ It sounds like

  • Short, declarative. Hard nouns: dirt, lip, loam, patch, yard, send.
  • Two-beat rhythm — "Pack it. Send it."
  • Humor over aggression (S&M's pranks, Sunday's warmth).
  • Drops a Portuguese line mid-English, no translation.
  • Shows the unglamorous: the rained-out lip, the re-pack.
  • Invite-by-labor: welcoming, never gatekeeping.

    • ✕ It never sounds like

  • Spec sheets — "23% lighter carbon layup."
  • Hype-bro caps-lock aggression.
  • Motivational-poster vagueness — "CHASE YOUR DREAMS."
  • Polished agency gloss or corporate fluff.
  • Exclamation-point spam.
  • Gatekeeping cruelty (the surf-localism trap).

    The Merch Ladder

    Lead with identity items, not a tee catalog. The brand is literally named Patcho — patches are your signature SKU and cost almost nothing. Climb the ladder only as demand proves out. Start zero-inventory (print-on-demand), graduate to local batch for the high-margin stuff.

    RUNG 01
    Stickers
    Free in every order & at the spot. Pure spread.
    ~90%margin
    RUNG 02 ★
    Patches
    Your namesake + membership token. The signature drop.
    ~80%margin
    RUNG 03
    Caps · Pins · Beanies
    Cheap-to-mid hype with strong identity pull.
    ~55%margin
    RUNG 04
    Tees
    Real revenue per unit — once a design proves demand.
    ~50%margin
    RUNG 05
    Hoodies
    Highest ticket. The reward for an audience that's bought in.
    ~45%margin

    Model → limited drops (window + count + end date), not an always-on catalog. Global fans: Fourthwall / print-on-demand. Brazil: Nuvemshop/Yampi + Pix + Mercado Pago + Correios; look at MEI once revenue starts. Stickers & patches batch-printed locally from day one.

    08

    The 90-Day Launch

    The core thesis from the growth research: you're not starting a clothing brand — you're filming a story that happens to sell patches. The proven MTB path is a two-person crew filming an episodic backyard build + progression. You already have all three pieces: the jungle trail, the foam pit, and a couple. Build the tribe first; the product follows.

    Weeks 1–2FOUNDATION
    • Run Namechk on @ridepatcho / @somospatcho / backups; claim the winner identically on IG, TikTok, YouTube; register the matching .com.
    • Lock the crew name + 2–3 insider terms. Write the one-paragraph "what Patcho is."
    • Order a small local batch of stickers + your first patch — something physical, immediately.
    Weeks 3–6START THE ENGINE
    • Film one big batch weekend: dig session + ride + foam-pit progression.
    • Publish Episode 1 of "The Backyard Build" (long-form) + extract 3–5 vertical shorts from it.
    • Post 2–3 shorts/week. Hook in the first 3 seconds — open on the send, never on a logo. Seed your first 100–500 from your own networks + Pinkbike/Reddit.
    Weeks 7–10FIND YOUR FORMAT
    • Run named series weekly: Backyard Build · Foam to Dirt · Wipeout Wednesday · His & Hers (the couple = your differentiator).
    • Use Instagram Trial Reels to test hooks on non-followers; double down on anything clearing 60% hold.
    • Give patches/stickers to your crew — start seeding the membership identity.
    Weeks 11–13FIRST DROP + JAM
    • Open a tight first drop: patches + stickers + one tee, limited window. (Fourthwall global; add Nuvemshop/Pix if BR demand shows.)
    • Host a small jam at the spot — film it as your community + content centerpiece. Seeds your cinematic hero piece for later.
    • Start the annual ritual seed: a named backyard jam/award only Patcho gives out (the "SOTY" move — minting legitimacy beats selling product).

    Honest expectations → first 100 subs in ~8–12 weeks; first 1,000 in 6–12 months. ~90% of channels never hit 1,000 — almost always from quitting before the compounding starts. The discipline that decides it: niche to the backyard-build story, and don't stop before month 12.

    09

    If You Asked Me

    You don't have to pick one purebred. Here's the blend I'd build first — and it's reversible.

    Recommended starting blend

    Foam Jungle color · El Parche patch · Yard Dog speed

    Lead with the Foam Jungle palette (your unowned green-clay lane) and the El Parche patch as the core identity object — that's the membership moat and it's literally your name. But launch at Yard Dog speed: stickers and a sharpie-simple wordmark first, because they cost nothing and signal "core" instantly. Keep the creature in your back pocket as a secondary mascot for shovels, jump lips and kids' stuff — it's the distinctiveness bet nobody else in MTB is making. Heritage/Track 78 is the look to grow into, not start with.

    The throughline across every direction: Patcho is a story you film and a crew people want to join. The patches are how they join.
